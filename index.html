The Treatment

What Is
Hyaluronic Acid?

Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring substance in the skin that plays a key role in hydration, elasticity, and volume. In aesthetic medicine, it is used as a non-surgical treatment to restore volume, smooth wrinkles, enhance facial contours, and improve overall skin quality.

Treatments are individually planned based on facial anatomy, tissue characteristics, and aesthetic goals. Depending on the indication, hyaluronic acid can define facial contours, soften lines and folds, restore volume loss, or improve skin hydration and texture.

At our clinic in Lisbon, every treatment is individually planned and performed exclusively by board-certified plastic surgeons, with a strong focus on safety, anatomical precision, and natural-looking, long-lasting results.

Common Questions

Frequently
Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before booking your hyaluronic acid consultation.

Does the treatment hurt? +
Injections are administered with fine needles or cannulas and are generally well tolerated. Most hyaluronic acid products contain a local anaesthetic to minimise discomfort. Mild sensitivity at injection sites may persist for a short time after treatment.
How long do results last? +
Duration varies by treatment area and product used. In general, results last between 9 and 18 months. Areas with more movement (such as the lips) metabolise filler faster than structural areas like the cheeks or temples.
Is there any downtime? +
Downtime is minimal. Most patients return to their normal daily activities immediately after treatment. Temporary swelling, redness, or small bruises at injection sites may occur but usually resolve within a few days.
Can the filler be reversed? +
Yes. Hyaluronic acid fillers are fully reversible using an enzyme called hyaluronidase, which rapidly dissolves the product if a correction or removal is ever required. This is one of the key safety advantages of hyaluronic acid over permanent fillers.
Can it be combined with other treatments? +
Yes. Hyaluronic acid is frequently combined with Wrinkle treatment with toxin, thread lifts, or skin rejuvenation treatments for a more comprehensive facial result. Your surgeon will advise on the most effective combination during consultation.
